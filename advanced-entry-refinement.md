# Advanced Entry Refinement - OTE + Opening Gaps

## Overview

Advanced entry refinement combines **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)**, **New Week Opening Gap (NWOG)**, and **New Day Opening Gap (NDOG)** to identify precise entry zones within larger setups. These tools help traders avoid entering too early or too late within Premium/Discount arrays.

---

## Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

### Definition

OTE refers to the **62-79% retracement zone** of a swing leg (measured from swing low to swing high for bullish, or swing high to swing low for bearish). ICT emphasizes the **70.5% level** as the "sweet spot" within this range.

### OTE Levels

| Retracement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **62%** | Minimum OTE level (aligns with classic 0.618 Fibonacci) |
| **70.5%** | Optimal/sweet spot - ICT's preferred entry zone |
| **79%** | Maximum OTE level |

### How to Use OTE

1. **Identify the swing leg** you're measuring from (e.g., Asian low to London high)
2. **Draw Fibonacci retracement** from the origination point to the swing terminus
3. **Watch for price to enter the 62-79% zone**, ideally targeting 70.5%
4. **Look for confirming price action** within the OTE zone:
   - Fair Value Gap formation
   - Order block formation
   - Inducement/liquidity sweep
   - Market structure shift

### OTE Entry Rules

**Bullish OTE (after bearish swing):**
- Measure from swing high (100%) to swing low (0%)
- Wait for 62-79% retracement UP into the zone
- Enter on reversal confirmation at 70.5% or within the zone

**Bearish OTE (after bullish swing):**
- Measure from swing low (100%) to swing high (0%)
- Wait for 62-79% retracement DOWN into the zone
- Enter on reversal confirmation at 70.5% or within the zone

### Example: Bullish OTE

```
Scenario: NQ creates swing low at 15,000 during Asia, rallies to 15,200 in London, now retracing

Swing High: 15,200 (100%)
Swing Low: 15,000 (0%)
Range: 200 points

OTE Levels:
- 62% = 15,124
- 70.5% = 15,141
- 79% = 15,158

Action: Wait for price to retrace to 15,141 area, look for bullish FVG or order block, enter long targeting continuation to take liquidity above 15,200
```

## New Week Opening Gap (NWOG)

### Definition

The **NWOG** is the price gap between:
- **Friday's closing price** (settlement at 4:00 PM ET)
- **Sunday's opening price** (futures reopen at 6:00 PM ET)

This gap often acts as a magnetic price level throughout the week.

### NWOG Characteristics

- Creates a **visible gap** on the chart between Friday close and Sunday open
- Acts as a **Fair Value Gap** that price often returns to fill
- Can serve as **support or resistance** throughout the week
- Most significant when the gap is **substantial** (20+ points on NQ)

### NWOG Trading Strategy

**Gap Down (Sunday opens below Friday close):**
1. NWOG zone = area between Friday close (top) and Sunday open (bottom)
2. Expect price to rally back UP into the gap to "fill" it
3. **First scenario:** Price fills the gap early in the week, then reverses (gap acts as resistance)
4. **Second scenario:** Price fills the gap and continues through it (gap acts as support on retest)

**Gap Up (Sunday opens above Friday close):**
1. NWOG zone = area between Friday close (bottom) and Sunday open (top)
2. Expect price to decline back DOWN into the gap to "fill" it
3. **First scenario:** Price fills the gap early in the week, then reverses (gap acts as support)
4. **Second scenario:** Price fills the gap and continues through it (gap acts as resistance on retest)

### NWOG Key Concepts

- **Gap fill is expected** - price has a high probability of returning to fill the gap within the week
- **Reaction at gap** - observe how price reacts when entering the NWOG zone
- **Partial fills** - gap may only partially fill before reversing
- **Multiple tests** - price may test the NWOG multiple times throughout the week

### Example: NWOG Gap Down

```
Scenario: NQ closes Friday at 15,300, opens Sunday at 15,250

NWOG Zone: 15,250 - 15,300 (50-point gap down)

Weekly Bias: Bullish (targeting 15,400)

Strategy:
- Monday-Tuesday: Watch for rally back into 15,250-15,300 NWOG zone
- Entry: If price fills the gap and shows bullish reaction (FVG, order block), enter long
- Target: Weekly high or next draw on liquidity at 15,400
- Stop: Below Sunday's opening low

Alternative: If price rejects at NWOG upper boundary (15,300), consider this resistance for short-term bearish play
```

## New Day Opening Gap (NDOG)

### Definition

The **NDOG** is the price gap between:
- **Previous day's closing price** (4:00 PM ET settlement)
- **Current day's opening price** (6:00 PM ET futures reopen)

NDOGs occur daily and provide intraday reference points.

### NDOG Characteristics

- Created **every day** when futures reopen
- Smaller magnitude than NWOG typically
- Provides **intraday targets** and reversal zones
- Most relevant during the **first session** (Asia/London)

### NDOG Trading Strategy

**Daily Gap Down:**
1. NDOG zone = area between yesterday's close (top) and today's open (bottom)
2. Expect potential rally to fill the gap during Asia or London session
3. Use gap fill as potential resistance or continuation zone

**Daily Gap Up:**
1. NDOG zone = area between yesterday's close (bottom) and today's open (top)
2. Expect potential decline to fill the gap during Asia or London session
3. Use gap fill as potential support or continuation zone

### NDOG Key Concepts

- **Session alignment** - NDOG is most relevant during overnight and London sessions
- **Quick fills** - NDOGs often fill faster than NWOGs (within first few hours)
- **New York override** - NDOG significance diminishes once New York session begins with strong momentum
- **Confluence with session opens** - NDOG combined with Asia/London range provides powerful reference

### Example: NDOG Gap Up

```
Scenario: NQ closes Monday at 15,280, opens Tuesday at 15,310

NDOG Zone: 15,280 - 15,310 (30-point gap up)

Daily Bias: Bearish (targeting Monday's low at 15,250)

Strategy:
- Asia Session: Watch for decline back into 15,280-15,310 NDOG zone
- Entry: If price fills gap and shows bearish reaction (breakdown, bearish FVG), enter short
- Target: Monday's low at 15,250 or previous day's discount array
- Stop: Above Asia session high

Alternative: If price holds above NDOG (15,310) and shows strength, gap becomes support for bullish continuation
```

## Confluence Scenarios: Combining OTE + Gaps

### Power Setup 1: OTE Aligns with NWOG

**Scenario:**
- Weekly bias is bullish
- Price creates swing low Monday, rallies Tuesday-Wednesday
- Thursday, price retraces into 70.5% OTE zone
- The OTE zone **overlaps with the NWOG** from the previous week

**Why It's Powerful:**
- Two independent confluences (OTE retracement + gap fill magnetism)
- Increases probability of reversal at this zone
- Provides clear entry with defined risk

**Entry Strategy:**
1. Wait for price to enter the OTE + NWOG overlap zone
2. Look for bullish confirmation (FVG, CHoCH, inducement)
3. Enter long with stop below the overlap zone
4. Target weekly high or next draw on liquidity

### Power Setup 2: OTE Aligns with NDOG

**Scenario:**
- Daily bias is bearish
- Price creates swing high in London, declines into New York
- New York close creates NDOG for the next day
- Next day's Asia session rallies into OTE zone that **aligns with NDOG**

**Why It's Powerful:**
- Intraday precision combining overnight gap with Fibonacci retracement
- High-probability reversal zone for same-day scalps
- Clear invalidation if price breaks through both levels

**Entry Strategy:**
1. Identify the OTE zone from the previous day's swing
2. Note the NDOG from previous close to current open
3. If they overlap, wait for price to enter the confluence zone
4. Enter on bearish confirmation (bearish FVG, breakdown of structure)
5. Target previous day's low or discount array

### Power Setup 3: Triple Confluence (OTE + NWOG + NDOG)

**Scenario:**
- Weekly bias is bullish, targeting previous week's high
- NWOG exists from last week (gap down)
- Current day's NDOG also exists (gap down)
- Intraday swing creates OTE zone that **overlaps with both gaps**

**Why It's Ultimate Setup:**
- Three independent technical confluences
- Extremely high probability of reaction
- Clear entry, stop, and target framework

**Entry Strategy:**
1. Confirm all three elements align in the same price zone
2. Wait for price to enter the triple confluence zone
3. Watch for explosive reaction (strong reversal, large FVG formation)
4. Enter aggressively with tight stop just outside the zone
5. Target aggressive profit levels (weekly high, major liquidity pool)

**Example: Triple Confluence Long**

```
Setup:
- NWOG: 15,200-15,220 (gap down from previous week)
- NDOG: 15,210-15,225 (gap down from yesterday)
- OTE 70.5%: 15,215 (from Monday low to Wednesday high)

Overlap Zone: 15,210-15,220 (all three confluences present)

Friday Asia Session:
- Price retraces from 15,280 down into 15,210-15,220 zone
- Forms bullish FVG at 15,212
- Creates CHoCH (change of character) breaking above recent lower high

Entry: Long at 15,218 (within the confluence zone, after CHoCH)
Stop: 15,205 (below the zone and FVG)
Target: 15,300 (previous week's high, weekly draw on liquidity)
Risk:Reward: 13 points risk / 82 points reward = 6.3:1 RR
```

## OTE + Gap Integration Checklist

Before taking a trade using OTE or gaps, verify:

- [ ] **Higher timeframe bias is clear** (weekly/daily direction established)
- [ ] **OTE zone is identified** (62-79%, preferably targeting 70.5%)
- [ ] **NWOG is mapped** (if trading early in the week)
- [ ] **NDOG is mapped** (if trading intraday)
- [ ] **Confluence exists** (OTE + gap overlap increases probability)
- [ ] **Confirming price action present** (FVG, order block, CHoCH, inducement)
- [ ] **Clear invalidation level** (stop loss placement below/above the zone)
- [ ] **Target is identified** (liquidity pool, session high/low, previous swing)

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Entering at 50% Instead of OTE
**Mistake:** Taking entries at the 50% retracement (equilibrium) instead of waiting for deeper OTE zone.

**Why It Fails:** 50% is often just a pause before price continues to the true reversal zone at 62-79%.

**Solution:** Be patient. Wait for price to reach the OTE zone before entering.

### 2. Ignoring Gap Context
**Mistake:** Trading gaps in isolation without considering weekly/daily bias.

**Why It Fails:** Gaps may fill, but the reaction depends on higher timeframe direction.

**Solution:** Only trade gap fills that align with your higher timeframe bias.

### 3. Forcing OTE on Every Swing
**Mistake:** Measuring OTE on every minor swing instead of significant session-based legs.

**Why It Fails:** Creates noise and false signals. Not every swing is algorithmically significant.

**Solution:** Measure OTE only on **major session swings** (Asia low to London high, London high to NY close, etc.).

### 4. No Confirmation Required
**Mistake:** Entering immediately when price touches OTE or gap zone without waiting for confirmation.

**Why It Fails:** Price can slice through the zone without reacting if no valid setup exists.

**Solution:** Always wait for confirmation: FVG, order block, CHoCH, or inducement within the zone.

### 5. Overtrading NDOG
**Mistake:** Trading every single NDOG every day regardless of context.

**Why It Fails:** Not all NDOGs are significant. Small gaps (<10 points on NQ) may not provide meaningful reactions.

**Solution:** Focus on **substantial NDOGs** (20+ points) that align with higher timeframe bias and session narratives.

## Summary

**OTE (Optimal Trade Entry):**
- 62-79% retracement zone, with 70.5% as the sweet spot
- Used to refine entries within larger discount/premium arrays
- Requires confirmation via FVG, order block, or structure shift

**NWOG (New Week Opening Gap):**
- Gap between Friday close and Sunday open
- Acts as weekly magnet and support/resistance
- High probability of fill within the week

**NDOG (New Day Opening Gap):**
- Gap between previous day's close and current day's open
- Provides intraday reference and reversal zones
- Most relevant during Asia/London sessions

**Power of Confluence:**
- Combining OTE + NWOG or OTE + NDOG increases probability
- Triple confluence (OTE + NWOG + NDOG) is the ultimate high-probability setup
- Always align with higher timeframe bias and wait for confirmation

**Key Principle:**
Use OTE and gaps as **refinement tools** within your broader trading narrative. They are not standalone strategies but precision entry techniques that work best when combined with bias, session analysis, and liquidity targeting.
